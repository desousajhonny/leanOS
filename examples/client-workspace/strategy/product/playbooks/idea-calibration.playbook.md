# Idea Calibration

## Purpose

Move from a raw founder idea to a confirmed Strategy Baseline without jumping into roadmap, MVP scope or delivery.

## Inputs

- ../../../leanos.yaml
- ../knowledge/brief.md
- ../knowledge/problem.md
- ../knowledge/icp.md
- ../knowledge/value-proposition.md
- ../knowledge/positioning.md
- ../knowledge/business-model-canvas.md
- ../knowledge/validation-notes.md

## Process

1. Load the Product AGENT and choose Product Strategist or Product Manager.
2. Use `skills/map-business-baseline/SKILL.md` to summarize known context, business stage and Strategy Baseline gaps.
3. Clarify product, ICP, problem and value proposition only where the baseline is weak.
4. Use business-model work only when pricing, revenue, delivery model or channel assumptions affect the initial thesis.
5. Separate facts, assumptions, open questions and founder decisions.
6. Present the calibrated idea as a concise Strategy Baseline.
7. Ask the founder to confirm, correct or keep calibrating before writing knowledge files.
8. After confirmation, offer the bridge to `playbooks/mvp-validation-scope.playbook.md`; do not create roadmap, Epics, Features or delivery scope here.

## Guided Conversation

Use `../../../ai-standard/foundation/guided-conversation.md`.

- Ask one useful question at a time, tied to the biggest Strategy Baseline gap.
- Avoid interview fatigue; do not force every skill when the baseline is already clear enough.
- Use numbered choices only when they make the founder's decision easier.
- Let the founder answer with a number or free-form text.
- End with a clear confirmation question before file updates.

Do not ask a rigid questionnaire. Ask only what is missing.

## Output

- Calibrated idea summary
- Strategy Baseline proposal
- Known facts
- Assumptions
- Open questions
- Recommended next step toward MVP Validation Scope
